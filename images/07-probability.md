## Figures not generated (implied by examples in text)

The chapter references visual concepts that need diagrams but are described in prose:

1. **Multiplication rule grid (cupcake example):** 3 cake choices × 4 frosting choices = 12 cells. Each cell is one outcome.

2. **Tree diagram for dependent draws:** Two stages, where the second stage's branches vary depending on the first stage's outcome. Example: drawing cards without replacement.

3. **Complement illustration:** A rectangle (sample space S) with a subset highlighted (event E) and the surrounding region shaded (complement E').

4. **Roulette wheel schematic:** A wheel divided into 38 equal sectors, with 18 red, 18 black, 2 green (0 and 00). Shows why "red or black" is not a fair 50-50 bet.

5. **Expected value table:** Shows outcomes, their values, probabilities, and products for a simple game (e.g., roll a die). Demonstrates the summation formula.
