# Images — Chapter 5: Algebra

## Figure locations and brief descriptions

All figures below would be drawn or sourced from the OpenStax materials. The rewrite preserves the mathematical content but integrates figures into the flow rather than treating them as standalone.

### Concept 2 (Functions & Graphing)

**Figure 1:** Coordinate plane with labeled axes and quadrants.
- Shows x-axis (horizontal, labeled "independent variable") and y-axis (vertical, labeled "dependent variable").
- Marks origin at (0, 0).
- Quadrants labeled I–IV, counterclockwise from upper right.
- Example points plotted: $(2, 3)$, $(-1, 2)$, $(3, -2)$, $(-2, -1)$.
- Purpose: teach how to read and plot ordered pairs.

**Figure 2:** Graph of pizza cost function $f(t) = 12 + 1.5t$.
- Points: $(0, 12)$, $(1, 13.50)$, $(2, 15)$, $(3, 16.50)$, $(4, 18)$.
- A straight line (linear function) rising with positive slope.
- Y-intercept at $(0, 12)$ marked with a dot and label.
- Purpose: visualize that inputs (number of toppings) map to outputs (cost).

**Figure 3:** Line $y = 2x + 3$ with intercepts marked.
- Line crosses x-axis at $(-1.5, 0)$ (x-intercept labeled).
- Line crosses y-axis at $(0, 3)$ (y-intercept labeled).
- Two points clearly marked; line drawn through them.
- Purpose: show intercepts as special points.

**Figure 4:** Slope visualization using a right triangle.
- Two points on a line, e.g., $(0, 5)$ and $(3, 3)$.
- Right triangle drawn with horizontal leg (run = 3) and vertical leg (rise = -2).
- Slope $m = \frac{-2}{3}$ labeled.
- Purpose: explain rise/run and show slope is negative for a downward line.

### Concept 3 (Systems of Equations)

**Figure 5:** Two lines intersecting on a coordinate plane.
- Line 1: $2t + b = 8.50$ (or equivalently $b = 8.50 - 2t$).
- Line 2: $t + 2b = 9$ (or equivalently $b = 4.5 - 0.5t$).
- Intersection point marked at approximately $(2.66, 3.17)$.
- Labels show "solution" at the intersection.
- Purpose: visualize that a system's solution is where graphs meet.

**Figure 6:** Two parallel lines (no intersection).
- Line 1: $y = 2x + 3$.
- Line 2: $y = 2x + 5$.
- Both lines have the same slope (2) but different y-intercepts.
- Text label: "No solution: parallel lines never meet."
- Purpose: illustrate a system with no solution.

**Figure 7:** One line drawn twice (same line).
- Both equations $2x + y = 5$ and $4x + 2y = 10$ represent the same line.
- The line is drawn once; both labels point to it.
- Text label: "Infinitely many solutions: the equations are the same line."
- Purpose: show a system with infinitely many solutions.

### Integration (optional figure)

**Figure 8:** Pizza shop scenario with two pizzas.
- Two pizza circles, one labeled "Margherita" ($12 + 1t$), one labeled "Supreme" ($16 + 0.5t$).
- Toppings illustrated on each.
- Cost equation and constraint shown nearby.
- Purpose: visualize the concrete scenario before solving algebraically.
