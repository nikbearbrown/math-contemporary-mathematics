## How to Create Diagrams for Typesetting

### Line, Ray, and Angle Diagrams

**Cold Open Visualization:** A surveyor with transit at a property corner. Show:
- A stake at the starting point (the vertex)
- A horizontal line extending to the right (one ray)
- A line extending upward at 47° (the second ray)
- An arc indicating the 47° angle between them
- A second line perpendicular to the horizontal (showing 90°)

**Perpendicular and Parallel:** Two parallel vertical lines with a horizontal transversal crossing both. Mark the perpendicularity of the transversal to the vertical lines with small squares at intersections.

### Polygon and Area Diagrams

**Rectangular Kitchen:** A top-down view of a kitchen floor (12 ft × 9 ft). Label all sides. Show a dotted line around the perimeter (for the trim measurement). Show the interior divided into 1-square-foot tiles (108 total).

**Circle Diagrams:** Show a circle with radius $r$ and diameter $d = 2r$ clearly labeled. Show the circumference as a boundary distance. Show a second circle with the interior shaded and divided into approximate square units to visualize area.

### Right Triangle Diagrams

**Pythagorean Theorem Visualization:** 
- A right triangle with legs labeled $a$ and $b$, hypotenuse $c$
- A small square at the vertex indicating the 90° angle
- Squares constructed on each side showing areas $a^2$, $b^2$, and $c^2$
- The equation $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ displayed prominently

**Surveyor's Triangle (Pond Example):**
- Point $A$ on one side of a rectangular pond
- Point $C$ perpendicular to the far shore, 60 m away from $A$
- Point $B$ directly across the pond, 80 m from $C$ along the shore
- The right angle marked at $C$
- The hypotenuse $AB$ (100 m) drawn and labeled as "Direct distance across pond"

## Instructional Notes for Figure Use

When typesetting:

1. **Use consistent colors and line weights** across related diagrams. For example, all right angles should be marked the same way (small square).

2. **Label all vertices, sides, and angles clearly.** Use capital letters for points, lowercase for lengths.

3. **Include a scale reference** on diagrams involving real-world measurements (e.g., "1 cm = 10 meters").

4. **Show work explicitly** in area and perimeter diagrams. For the kitchen example, annotate the perimeter calculation ($2 \times 12 + 2 \times 9 = 42$) directly on the figure.

5. **Grounding matters:** Show real applications (actual buildings, rooms, plots of land) rather than abstract shapes. The 12 ft × 9 ft kitchen should look like a kitchen you could stand in.
